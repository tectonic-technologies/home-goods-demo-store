# MARLOW — house brand for the home-goods demo store

Demo house brand built from a de-branded blend of elevated, warm-minimalist modern
home goods (tabletop, textiles, decor, lighting, small storage). Internal Spectrum
demo store only. Name is swappable.

## Positioning
A more thoughtful home. Considered everyday objects, made to be lived with — fewer,
better pieces in natural materials. Mid-to-premium, never precious.

## Voice
- Quiet, domestic, unfussy. Warm but restrained. No hype, no exclamation marks,
  no "revolutionary" or "must-have".
- Lowercase-leaning in UI display; sentence case in body copy.
- Tactile and precise. Lead with the material and how it lives in a home, not stacked
  adjectives.
- Honest about material and care. Never fabricate claims or origin stories.

## Naming convention (functional / minimal)
- Drop proprietary marketing prefixes and ®/™.
- Keep the functional descriptor: material + form + category.
- Examples: "Stoneware Dinner Plate", "Linen Duvet Cover", "Ribbed Glass Tumbler",
  "Waffle Bath Towel", "Hand-Thrown Vase", "Brass Table Lamp".

## Aesthetic (drives the home theme restyle)
- Palette: warm stone neutrals (bone, oat, clay, sand), near-black ink, one muted
  accent (faded sage or terracotta).
- Materials story: stoneware, linen, organic cotton, warm wood, simple glass, aged brass.
- Type: a fine high-contrast serif for display + a clean grotesque sans for UI/body.
- Layout: generous white space, calm grids, restrained motion.
- Photography: soft daylight, real tabletop and bed scenes, minimal props, lived-in.

## Content rules (carry into all store copy)
- No em-dashes in rendered text. Use commas, periods, or middots.
- No numeric counters / "N of M" in UI.
- No fabricated scarcity; FOMO signals must map to real (synthetic-but-consistent) data.
- Care and dimensions are facts, state them plainly (dishwasher-safe, machine-washable,
  measured in inches).
